Finding Your First Race

Picking your first ultra doesn't have to be overwhelming. The right race isn't necessarily the most famous one or the hardest one. It's the one that fits where you are right now and gives you the best shot at crossing the finish line. Start smart, finish strong, and save the big buckle races for later.

What to Look for in a First Ultra

When you're new to the distance, the course setup matters more than you might think. Look for races with frequent aid stations, a manageable cutoff time, and a course that's either a loop or an out-and-back. These formats are much easier for your crew and pacers to navigate, which means you'll have more support when you need it most. A well-organized first race can make the difference between a great experience and a miserable one.

The Vernal Equinox — A Personal Favorite

The Vernal Equinox was my first ultra and I still think it's one of the best options out there for beginners. The course is set up as a loop, which means your crew can stay in one spot and see you multiple times throughout the race. If you're running self-supported, that same setup makes it easy to stash a drop bag and resupply without relying on anyone else. The terrain is manageable, the atmosphere is welcoming, and the race organization is solid. It's exactly the kind of environment where a first-timer can thrive.

What to Expect on Race Day

Your first ultra will feel different from anything you've done before, and that's okay. Start slower than you think you need to. Eat before you're hungry and drink before you're thirsty. Be friendly at aid stations — the volunteers are there to help you and a good attitude goes a long way. Somewhere around the halfway point things will probably get hard. That's normal. Keep moving, keep eating, and remember that every person out there started exactly where you are right now.
